# Research Ops

Structured deep research that produces decisions, not just information.

## Research Modes

**Market Research** — Size, trends, key players, gaps, opportunities. Used before entering a new niche or pitching a new service at JF Web Studios.

**Lead Intelligence** — Deep dive on a specific prospect before outreach. Company history, online presence, pain points, decision makers, best angle for a pitch.

**Competitor Analysis** — What are other web agencies doing in Houston? What are their prices, services, weaknesses? Where is the gap?

**Trading Research** — Fundamental and technical context on a ticker. Earnings, news catalysts, sector trends, key levels.

**Industry Intel** — Oil & gas industry news, trends, regulatory changes. Relevant for Jhonnatan's day job and potential industry-specific web clients.

## Research Process

1. **Define the question** — What specific decision will this research inform?
2. **Source matrix** — Pull from web search, news, financial data, and domain knowledge
3. **Synthesize** — Cut noise, surface the signal
4. **Actionable output** — End with: what does this mean, and what's the move?

## Output Format

Every research output ends with:
- *Key finding* — The single most important thing learned
- *Risk* — What could make this wrong
- *Recommended action* — Exactly what to do with this information

## Principles

- No fluff summaries — if it doesn't change a decision, it doesn't get included
- Always cite the source or method used
- Flag when data is stale or incomplete
- First-principles thinking over trend-following
